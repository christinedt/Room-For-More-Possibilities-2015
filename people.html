This talent unit is about bringing together design thinking, technological imagination with empathy to what makes people human and idealistic - disruptive human faculty that giving us ability to imagine that things could be different.




Wojtek Szumowski

Director

I am interested in experimenting with transitions between the virtual and the physical as new storytelling space.The most intriguing thing about the digital and real spaces is the people.

I joined Crispin Porter + Bogusky in 2004 to innovate creative strategy process and discover new cultural possibilities for some of the most beloved brands like Volkswagen, Virgin Atlantic, IKEA, Hulu, Burger King, Coke ZERO, Pearl Izumi, American Express, DIESEL, Milka, Infiniti and many others.

Email Tweet

Room for More Possibilities is an agile invention process seeking new forms to bring together technology, design, creative strategy, story-making, fresh set of values and disruptive empathy to what makes people human.




Alexia Mathieu

Invention Strategist

Unlike other strategists, I don’t build future scenarios to predict how we will use technology tomorrow. I conceptualize the future to create a more human and emotional relationship with technology today.

In London, I discovered my passion for observing how technology shapes our lives. At Central Saint Martins, this is where I developed my recipe for imagining future scenarios and investigated what lies ahead for our love relationship with technology. I arrived in Boulder to kick start a small Room For More Possibilities at CP+B. where I experimented with creative technologists and designers to create new storytelling forms.

Email Tweet

I believe in a more poetic future between people and technology. Each project, for me, is the opportunity to examine how people use, misuse, love, trust, hate, and disrupt technology to inspire different kind of futures.




Monique Grimord

Invention Designer

As a rapid prototyper of the future, I use my background in the social sciences and conceptual design to bring new ways of being human into an object's form or into a designed system.

After completing an education in the social sciences and traditional design, I explored all other forms of visual communication through the alternative graphic design graduate program at Savannah College of Art and Design. There, I used the under-appreciated power of analog systems and prototypes to experiment with new ways for people to understand their relationships with data, shared space, and one another.

Email Tweet

Using a lot of cardboard, paint, and a little bit of electronics, I invent ways for an interaction to tell a story we haven't heard before.
